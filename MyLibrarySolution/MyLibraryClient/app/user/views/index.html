Classical period

The Library of Alexandria, in Egypt, was the largest and most significant great library of the ancient world.[12] It flourished under the patronage of the Ptolemaic dynasty and functioned as a major center of scholarship from its construction in the 3rd century BC until the Roman conquest of Egypt in 30 BC.
